## Overview

Accessing and managing medical records is a significant challenge for both patients and healthcare providers. Patients often
struggle with delays, incomplete information, and the inability to seamlessly share their records between providers.
This results in wasted time, repeated tests, and frustration for everyone involved.

For healthcare providers, the lack of standardized and efficient record-sharing systems hampers their ability to deliver timely and
effective care. Providers frequently encounter inconsistencies in data formats, delays in receiving
critical information, and administrative burdens that divert focus from patient care.

## Problem

These challenges are compounded by the growing expectation for digital, on-demand solutions
that prioritize security, accessibility, and usability. A system that resolves these pain points would reduce redundancies,
improve collaboration, and empower individuals to take control of their health journey.

By addressing these gaps, there is an opportunity to transform how health records are accessed and shared,
benefiting patients and providers alike.

## Technologies Used

- **Next.js**: For fast, server-rendered React applications.
- **Figma**: For creating and managing design assets and prototypes.
- **Styled-Components**: For styling React components with a modular, themable approach.
- **Storybook**: For building an interactive, documented component library.

## Challenges and Learnings

One key challenge was balancing the need for flexibility with the desire to maintain design consistency. The solution involved creating well-defined design tokens and establishing clear guidelines for when and how components could be customized. Additionally, setting up effective collaboration workflows between designers and developers using Figma and Git was a learning experience that greatly improved the process.

## Outcome

The design system is now actively used across multiple projects, leading to faster development cycles, fewer design inconsistencies, and improved collaboration between design and development teams. It has become a foundation for scaling our products efficiently while ensuring a cohesive user experience.

## Further Work

- **Gather User Feedback and Iterate:**: Actively collect feedback from early users (patients and providers). Use surveys, interviews, and analytics to identify pain points, usability issues, and feature gaps. Prioritize iterative improvements based on their input.
- **Develop Partnerships with Healthcare Providers:**: Build relationships with clinics, hospitals, and other healthcare organizations. Showcase the platform’s benefits and explore integration opportunities with their existing systems.
- **Ensure Regulatory Compliance and Security:**: Continuously audit the platform to meet healthcare regulations like PIPEDA in Canada. Strengthen security protocols to protect sensitive health data and build trust with users.
- **Focus on Marketing and Outreach:**: Launch targeted marketing campaigns to raise awareness among patients and healthcare professionals. Leverage social media, content marketing, and healthcare conferences to showcase the app's value.
